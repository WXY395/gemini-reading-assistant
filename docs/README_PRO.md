## What GRA Pro Actually Does

### Critical Condense — Decision Navigation, Not Compression

The "Condense" button doesn't shorten text. It **interrogates** it.

Every click triggers a structured audit:

- **Expose hidden logic gaps** — surface assumptions the response treats as given
- **Surface counter-intuitive factors** — identify what the response conveniently omits
- **Lock decision basepoints** — extract the leverage points that matter to *your* outcome

This is not a summary. This is a decision shield.

> *"The most dangerous information bias is the one you agree with."*

You can customize the condense prompt to match your domain — legal risk analysis, technical feasibility, financial impact, or any critical lens your work demands.

### Weighted Memory Recall — Lock Your North Star

Long conversations drift. GRA Pro prevents it with a **two-tier memory architecture**:

| Tier | Symbol | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Core Anchor** | Gold pin | Project basepoints that must never be lost. Placed at the top of every recall injection with `[CRITICAL PROJECT BASEPOINT]` annotation. |
| **Phase Consensus** | Blue pin | Stage-specific conclusions. Useful context, but expendable if the conversation evolves. |

**Click cycle:** Unpin → Phase (blue) → Core (gold) → Remove

When you hit "Recall", core anchors surface first. The AI doesn't get to forget your north star — no matter how long the conversation runs.

### Context Usage Meter — See the Cliff Before You Fall

A real-time progress bar calibrated to your Gemini plan (Flash 32K / Pro 128K / Ultra 1M):

- **Green zone** — full cognitive headroom
- **Yellow at 50%** — prepare a snapshot
- **Red at 75%** — active warning with one-click handoff

The handoff doesn't just copy text. It transfers your **memory pins, recent exchanges, and plan calibration** to a fresh conversation — maintaining cognitive continuity across sessions.

### Unlimited Store Search

Free version caps search at 20 results. Pro removes the limit entirely — search your full conversation history without truncation.

## Why Architects and Developers Need GRA Pro

If you make decisions based on AI output, you are exposed to **information bias by default**.

Large language models are optimized to sound correct, not to flag what they're uncertain about. They don't volunteer counter-arguments. They don't tell you when your premise contradicts something from 30 messages ago. They don't distinguish between a core project requirement and a throwaway example.

**GRA Pro exists to close that gap.**

It doesn't make Gemini smarter. It makes *you* harder to mislead.

- **Architects** use Critical Condense to stress-test design rationale before committing to a direction
- **Developers** use Weighted Recall to keep API contracts and architectural decisions anchored across refactoring sessions
- **Researchers** use the Usage Meter to know exactly when context degradation will start affecting output quality
- **Decision-makers** use custom condense prompts tuned to their risk domain — legal, financial, operational

This is base-point thinking: the discipline of knowing which facts must survive every conversation turn, and building systems to enforce it.
